# The Identity Ceiling

The ceiling you hit in business is often about identity, not strategy. From [[Michel Lieben]]'s personal experience: fired from 4 jobs, failed at 7 startups, lost $40K in savings.

## The Pattern

Michel's first [[ColdIQ]] client was $3K/month because "I needed the money." He kept underpricing himself because he didn't believe he was worth more. Every time revenue grew, he hit a wall that had nothing to do with tactics -- it was about who he thought he was.

## Breaking Through

Michel stopped drinking at 29, aiming to hit 7-figure net worth by 30. Eight months of sobriety became his most productive period. The combination of removing distractions and betting on himself broke through the identity ceiling.

This concept connects to [[Agency Growth Phases]] -- each phase requires not just a new playbook, but a new version of who you are as a founder.

## In Content Strategy

Michel uses this vulnerability as a recurring narrative in his [[Founder-Led Content]]. It builds trust through honesty about failure, which resonates more than pure tactical content.
