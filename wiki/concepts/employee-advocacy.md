# Employee Advocacy

Scaling content output through team members rather than relying on one or two founders.

## ColdIQ's Competition

[[ColdIQ]] ran an internal LinkedIn competition: 24 employees, 581 posts in 85 days, 43,000+ reactions, 28,000+ comments, 34,000+ new followers. Result: 27 new clients, $153K in new MRR. Prize pool was ~$15K.

Prize structure: $5K (1st), $2.5K (2nd), $1.5K (3rd), plus $500 for anyone who published 20+ times.

## V2 Rules (2026)

[[Michel Lieben]] revamped the scoring: 1 point/like, 2 points/comment, 5 points per 1,000 impressions, 10 points per publication. Multipliers: video = 2X, original content = 1.5X. Relative audience growth factored in for fairness to smaller accounts.

## Why It Works

"You can't publish 2+ posts/day without losing reach. But 24 people posting..." solves the frequency ceiling. Employee content resonates because most LinkedIn users aren't founders -- they relate more to practitioners sharing what works in the trenches. See [[Founder-Led Content]] for the evolution from founder-only to team-wide publishing.

This is a core component of [[Allbound]] -- the content layer scales through people, not just ad spend.
